Who I Am

It is often asked, "What is more important - time or money?" I believe that Steve Jobs answered the question best, "It's really clear that the most precious resource we all have is time." Over the course of my lifetime, I have learned that you can always make more money. You can't make more time.

I have dedicated my life to serving others and have given over fifteen years of my life to my career in medicine. I attended my local community college as a high school student. I attended the University of California, San Diego for my bachelor's degree in Biochemistry and Cell Biology, with a minor in Ethnic Studies. I obtained my doctorate degree at Des Moines University and completed my Family Practice residency at Glendale Adventist Medical Center in Pasadena. I served as a Lieutenant in the United States Navy immediately following residency graduation and worked as a Family Practice doctor for Sharp Mission Park and Scripps Coastal Medical Group in San Diego.

Over the past nine years, I have transitioned into a career in business telecommunications in order to find a lifestyle more conducive to a single mother raising three children. I have served in a pro-bono capacity as a doctor for a majority of that time. In alignment with my entrepreneurial spirit, I have created a non-profit organization, Blessed Beyond Belief, Inc., and two other for-profit corporations, Grace Enterprises and Unconventionalism, Inc..

Why I Am Attending IIT

The mission of Grace Enterprises is to use ingenuity and technology to ensure that our families are taken care of and create a culture that allows us pursue our higher purpose in life. One of the focus points of Blessed Beyond Belief, Inc. and Unconventionalism, Inc. is to increase awareness and provide services for those who are advocates of sexual abuse victims or those have been sexually abused. Other main focus points of the corporations are to protect children, create change agents in the world at large and bridge the gap through new and existing community outreach programs to help individuals thrive mentally, emotionally, physically, spiritually, professionally and financially, as well as to create an open dialogue within the community regarding issues that affect an individual's emotional, mental, spiritual well-being and to promote healing and growth in a manner that breaks down traditional religious, societal, stereotypical barriers. In order to accomplish these goals, I believe that a foundation in information technology will be a vital enhancement to my career goals and only serves to strengthen the success of my businesses.

The corporations, as well as some of the service options offered through the corporations, require web development knowledge and the creation of several social media applications.

It is for the reasons listed above that I am pursing a degree in information technology and management and am extremely excited about the entrepreneurial and innovative aspects of the degree. I plan to create several applications that will benefit individuals socially and medically through my corporations and believe that the Illinois Institute of Technology will play an integral role in the accomplishment of those goals, overall corporation goals, and in my life's mission to serve others.
